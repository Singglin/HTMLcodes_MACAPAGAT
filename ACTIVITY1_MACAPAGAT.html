My HTML Journey from Chaos to Cat Videos

Or, how I accidentally created a self-playing cat videos website (and almost lost my sanity)

Armed with boundless enthusiasm and zero coding experience , I embarked on my quest to conquer HTML.
Little did I know, my journey would be less "Lord of the Rings" and more "Three Stooges"

My first website, lovingly named "Brenda's Blossoming Boutique" , resembled a digital Jackson Pollock painting.
Tags were scattered like confetti, colors clashed like a sunset on Mars . and the navigation system could only be
described as "whimsical" (by a very generous browser)

Determined to redeem myself, I tackled a "simple project: a cat video gallery. What ensued was a hilarious
(and slightly terrifiying) ballet of misplacewd semicolons, , rogue line breaks, and images that dances uncontrollably
across the screen. At one point, my entire website transformed into an auto-playing cat video slideshow, much to
the amusement (and slightly concern) of my family.

After severasl meltdown, countless tutorials, and gallons of coffee. I finally managed to wrangle my code into submission.
My cat video gallery wan't exactly "elegant" , but it functioned (mostly) . And hey, at least I learned that even the clumsiest
coder can create something... interesting . So here's to Brenda's Blossoming Boutique . the self-playing cat video video extravaganza
and the never-ending quest to master the art of HTML!
